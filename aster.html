Aster Flower

Credit: The Flower Expert

"Aster" is actually the genus for starlike flowers that can be white, red, pink, purple, lavender, or blue. The yellow circles you see in a aster flower's center are actually many small, tubular flowers. Since the tubular flowers are usually a different colour from the petals, or ray flowers, it often appears to be one single flower with a circular head.

Aster flowers can grow all over the world, from the Americas to Europe and Asia. Since there are so many places, and thus varying species, the country will also have the specific species' name with it, if available.

Meanings

Country Meaning Source
Ancient Greece Sacred to the gods, it's believed that the goddess Astraea's tears turned into these flowers as they fell from the stars, and can scare away snakes and evil spirits. Flower Meaning
Native America Associated with a tale of two girls lost in the woods. Found by an old herablist, she saw their future and decided to turn them into flowers to spare them. The girl with a blue dress became an aster flower. Flower Meanings
