Sakura -- Japanese Flowering Tree

Prunus Serrulata

Credit: Neurons to Nebulae

These trees are native to Japan, with many variations. Their petals can range from pink to white, and even has festivals based around them. Plus, while you may not want to eat a large quantity of pure cherry blossom petals at once, both petals and bark can be used in various recipies, such as salted cherry blossom tea and sakura-mochi!
